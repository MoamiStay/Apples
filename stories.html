My Kin

Norway June 12. 1940

At 6AM sharp a beaming sun ray snuck its way in through a small crack in the wooden boards. I observed the boarded up window on the far side of the room through heavy eyelids. The sun was hard at work finding her way in through every little crack. My eyes were adjusted to the darkness and I could make out the the silhouettes of the things in my room. Among them I noticed there was a unfamiliar object. A deep breath worked its way up from my chest. It turned into one of those good yawns that just keeps building up and makes your eyes water just a tiny bit. Body stretching out and twisting back into a crooked position.

I sat up in my bed, the bed springs loudly declaring my awakened presence. If by any chance someone missed out on the bed spring choir, the squeaking floorboards would surely do the job. Considering it was only me and my mother, Elanora, living in the house I wasn't worried. Quite frankly, I wasn't sure my mother slept at all. I can't ever recall seeing her sleep. If she did, she'd be the kind to sleep with her eyes open. She had always been paranoid, looking behind her back with every two steps. However she wasn't always like this. I remember a time when she was a sweet and loving soul. Caring about the people close to her more than anything. Yet, I can't bring myself to blame her after what happened. It broke her, March 21. 1937.

As for Elmar, my father, he was always distant to both me and Elanora. To be honest, I never understood why my mother insisted on staying by his side as he practically treated us like strangers. It seemed like my fathers interest in us only lessened throughout the years. Still Elanora grew ever closer and dependent on him. Perhaps it was just in a futile attempt to bring him back.

No one really liked my father. I would describe him as arrogant. Someone who was feeding off of his own pride, but in a very subtle way. He was a man of few words, but his distaste for human kind was a recurring theme. And as far as I know, the only real friend he ever had was a cat which he even named after himself. Yet, he always seemed to want to have people around him, and people seemed to be oddly drawn to him in a way I that I just couldn't wrap my head around. Then again, I suppose just because people didn't like him, they also didn't hate him. Elmar was an intimidating man, and no one really dared to stand up against him. Besides, he never exactly hurt anyone or started any conflicts. He did, however, suffer from these occasional freak-outs. He would just suddenly start frantically crying, screaming and begging for some kind of release, yelling out strange words that made no sense to any man. Desperately clinging to people around him as if they could help him escape whatever hell he was trapped in. He was later diagnosed with Schizophrenia. March 21. 1937. That was the day he chose to end his life.

The creaking floorboards was revealing my every step towards the bathroom that was just down the hallway from my room. The sun shined through the window and reflected off the old, dusty pictures on the walls. With squinted eyes that was struggling to adjust to the sudden brightness I slowly waddled my way towards the bathroom door. Ruffling my hair, trying to figure out if taking a bath was really necessary. Nearing the end of the hallway, just as I was about to reach for the door handle I heard motorized vehicles and strange voices approaching.

I stopped for a moment to peek out the window. They came back again today. Just like they did yesterday. Three men got out of the car and approached the house. I heard three knocks on the door downstairs. Just like they did yesterday. I recognized one of the men, the one who seemed to be in charge. It was hard to make out the details of his face, but he was a tall and rather good looking man, probably in his late 30's. I heard muffled voices talking downstairs, it was a very brief and one-sided conversation which mostly sounded like gibberish to me. The only thing I could make sense out of was a "have a nice day ma'am" followed by a friendly chuckle. I stood by the window and observed them until they had all climbed on board the vehicle and was on their way, only leaving a dusty trail behind.

I proceeded with my usual morning routine, although I decided to skip the bath this day, instead I would be extra thorough the next day.

I walked down the creaking wooden staircase that sounded like it was all just about to come down. I greeted my mother who sat on a rusty chair by the kitchen window. Her yellow and grey tainted hair wavering from the slight breeze entering through the crack in the window. She didn't answer me, instead she continued to quietly mumble something to herself. The only words I could make out was "Elmar" and "baby". It wasn't uncommon for her to ramble like that. Spitting out a bunch of nonsense whenever she felt like it. She was fixated on one of the houseplants placed in the window frame. Her elbows was resting on the table while her skinny fingers were busy plucking all the leaves off the plant. She always did this whenever we had plants inside the house. She plucked away all the leaves and left only the flowers. I never question it.

- "Elmar is almost due, dear. Who will mother your child, dear? Who will mother your ugly child?"

She sounded spiteful.

-"Hm?"

- "Did you sleep well? You haven't had any nightmares lately?" I told her I slept well, and that I hadn't had any nightmares. I decided not to question the previous line of questions.

- "You aren't feeling sick are you? Doctor Holden just got home from his trip, should I get him to come over and do a check-up on you? I have saved some money, so I could-"

I cut her off before she could finish, trying to convince her I was fine and didn't need any doctors visit. It was always like this, every day I walked into a barrage of questions for a hundred new irrational fears and worries from my mother. For someone who was this poor at taking care of -and having responsibility for someone, or anything for that matter, she sure could've fooled me with all these questions.

- "There is nothing that has been eating away at you? Nothing that's been crawling under your skin?"

I opened the fridge and quickly scanned the contents. I shook my head in silence, my mother had been out doing errands and grocery shopping the day before, but she had only bought some pickled herring for herself and had forgotten to buy anything for me. I glanced over at the bread that was on the counter. Blue and green mold spots had started to form on the surface, and the sight put a quick end to my appetite.

- "What's wrong now? You've got to eat something. Where are you going? Are you leaving? "

I halted at the doorstep and leaned my hand on the wall right next to the photograph of mother and father on their wedding day 24 years ago. They both looked so young. It was difficult to tell whether they were happy or not. They were staring right into the camera; my father had a blank expressionless face and my mother wore a faint smile. I think it was supposed to be a smile anyway. I wonder if they were ever happy together.

I turned around to face her, she was no longer fixated on the poor plant that now only had a few green leaves left hanging. Her light blue eyes meeting my hazel green ones. Her tired skin and wrinkles didn't make her inquisitive gaze any less demanding. Then her eyes wandered over to the picture, and her eyes softened.

- "I loved him dearly. I miss him so much it pains me"

She had hardly ever spoken about her relationship to father. I'm not quite sure why, but hearing her say those words angered me.

- "Are you really that blind? He didn't care for you or me. He never loved you, yet you were too weak to realize it and leave"

- "Yes, and I hated him so much for it, I despised every moment together with him"

Suddenly there was hatred in her voice.

- "You aren't making any sense. Why didn't you leave then? Or at least let me leave?"

- "If only he had not been taken away from me"

She whimpered, and her eyes started to water. I decided to end the conversation there as I figured It wasn't gonna make me any more wiser.

My stomach growled. My appetite had slowly been creeping back.

- "There is no food. I'll just go get some bread and-"

- "What are you talking about? There is food in the fridge and bread on the bench. Don't try to make excuses to leave the house!"

I was really starting to get fed up with my mother at this point, and I couldn't hide the annoyance in my voice.

- "The bread is moldy, and I do not eat pickled herring. You know that, right?"

Elanora then slowly looked over at the bench with a slightly concerned look on her face. "Oh" she said quietly to herself as she turned back around to face the houseplant, ripping away the last few remaining leaves before she reached her hand out towards it and let her thumb carefully slide across the flower. Just as she was about to get up from the chair I spoke.

- "Why not let me go buy what we ne-"

- "NO! no, I'll do it. You can stay home, do the laundry if you're restless"

I hated living here. I hated living with her. She never let me leave the house or talk to anyone, and it had only gotten worse since the germans had invaded the country. I didn't even know if I had any other relatives left. I had tried to ask about them, but my mother didn't talk, and as far as I knew she was the only family I had.

I watched as my mother dragged her tired feet across the floor and headed towards the door, giving me one last warning look before she disappeared around the corner. I sat down beside the kitchen table, next to where she had been sitting, and looked out the window. The sun had now been taken prisoner by a flock of clouds that had suddenly appeared from behind the mountains. Putting a grey filter on the world outside the window. The leaves on the big oak tree in the backyard was swaying in the light breeze. I watched as a couple sparrows flew in with sticks, grass and straws for their nest, before heading back out in the search for more. Working tirelessly. My mind was just about to wander off, but was abruptly interrupted by a black raven that came seemingly out of nowhere and landed right outside of the window in front of me. I flinched back in my seat and watched it with wide eyes. I flinched again as it pecked on the glass, trying to get to the bread crumbs that was spilled on the table. The raven seemed to realize it was futile and let out a caw. It's head turning from side to side, it had noticed my presence and was now observing me through the window. The raven cawed one last time before taking off, the sound of the front door closing simultaneously. I leaned back in my chair and let out a sigh. I closed my eyes as I let my head fall back and I slowly rocked back and forth for a moment while letting my mind wander off.

Blood came trickling down around my feet. The left arm was dislocated and twisted behind the shoulder. Pools of blood started to form around me. One leg was torn off by the kneecap while the other was shattered from the knee to the foot. The road was now painted in a beautiful red. Her face was no longer recognizable. One eyeball popping out of the skull, while the other eye that was still intact was fixated on mine. Even though the jaw was broken and barely intact, there were sounds escaping her mouth. None that could be made out as words.

In her eye I saw true desperation and fear. She could only hope to find solice in death.
